Critical Reflection

How ChatGPT supported WP4

ChatGPT acted as a structured thinking partner across design, content, and technical layers. It helped generate the initial CSS foundations (reset.css, base.css, and layout.css), establish responsive breakpoints, and improve accessibility. I also used it to refine copy tone and clarify page structure. These uses supported faster iteration and deeper understanding of web standards and layout systems.

Responsible and ethical use

Following the Responsible Use of AI Tools Learning Document, I treated ChatGPT as a tool for exploration and validation, not authorship. No sensitive data were entered into prompts, and all responses were manually reviewed for factual accuracy, inclusivity, and accessibility. I refined or rewrote AI suggestions to ensure the outcomes reflected my intent and design rationale, aligning with course expectations for responsible GenAI practice.

Challenges and how I addressed them

  • Over-generalised suggestions: ChatGPT sometimes returned generic or verbose CSS or layout code. I countered this by testing outputs directly in my environment and simplifying them for performance and maintainability.
  • Voice and clarity: While AI edits improved grammar, they risked diluting my voice. I restored first-person and reflective phrasing to preserve authorship.
  • Accessibility validation: AI output required human review to confirm semantic accuracy, ARIA usage, and logical tab flow; I tested these in-browser with screen readers and keyboard navigation.

Evaluation strategies

  • Planned each prompt with a clear goal — to clarify, test, or scaffold — before engaging ChatGPT.
  • Applied critical review by verifying code validity, contrast ratios, and WCAG alignment before inclusion.
  • Maintained transparency through dated logs describing where AI assisted.
  • Reflected after each use to identify how the interaction contributed to efficiency, learning, and accessibility.

Looking forward

I will continue using GenAI tools for targeted support such as scaffolding, documentation drafting, and validation of accessibility logic, while prioritising human judgment and peer review. Maintaining a transparent workflow and documenting AI interactions will remain central to demonstrating responsible and reflective use.

Integrity Statement

I used GenAI tools to support—not replace—my learning and development in WP4. I critically reviewed, adapted, or rejected outputs, and the final website, rationale, and this acknowledgement reflect my own understanding and decisions. I have been transparent about how these tools contributed and I followed the assessment’s expectations for responsible use and academic integrity.
