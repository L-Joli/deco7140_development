Context and Purpose

Throughout the A3 Design Proposal, I utilised both Gemini and ChatGPT to support various stages of the design and development process. The primary purpose was to use these tools as dialogue partners to refine design concepts, accelerate front-end coding, and improve the clarity of written content. My goal was to leverage GenAI for brainstorming and implementation efficiency, allowing more time to focus on the strategic and ethical dimensions of the design work, rather than using it to replace my own critical thinking.

Critical Decision-Making and Ownership

I maintained ownership by treating GenAI outputs as starting points for further refinement. For instance, when using Gemini to redesign HTML sections, I critically evaluated the generated code structure and styling. While the initial layout was a useful foundation, I made significant modifications to ensure the design aligned with the project's specific aesthetic and accessibility requirements. Similarly, when ChatGPT suggested homepage content, I rewrote them to fit my narrative, ensuring the final voice was my own. I rejected any code or text that did not directly serve the project's goals or my design intent.

Accuracy, Bias, and Ethical Awareness

To ensure accuracy, all code generated by GenAI tools was tested for functionality. When I prompted for a color palette, I checked the functionality and the readability. I was aware that GenAI can produce generic content, so I actively refined text suggestions to be specific to the TechHub community, ensuring an inclusive tone that aligns with the client's mission.

Privacy and Sensitive Content

I adhered to strict privacy protocols throughout my use of GenAI. No sensitive information or proprietary details from the project briefs were ever used in my prompts. All queries were generalised to focus on design principles, coding techniques, or content ideas without disclosing confidential project context.

Reflection on Learning

Using GenAI tools significantly supported my learning by accelerating the more routine aspects of web development, such as boilerplate CSS and HTML. This efficiency gave me more cognitive space to engage deeply with UX principles, ethical frameworks, and accessibility standards. The process of prompting and refining also sharpened my ability to articulate design requirements clearly. I found that critically evaluating AI suggestions, identifying their strengths and weaknesses, was a powerful learning exercise in itself, reinforcing my understanding of what constitutes good design.

Attribution and Final Statement

I have used GenAI tools as a supportive element in my design and development workflow. All outputs were critically reviewed and edited. I maintained full control over all final design decisions, and the submitted work is a product of my own thinking, analysis, and creative judgment.
